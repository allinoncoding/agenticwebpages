Racial Disparities in Crime and Related Statistics

Homicide Victimization Rates (2020)

Source: CDC

Insight: Black individuals were homicide victims at a rate of 36.4 per 100,000 population, compared to 4.5 per 100,000 for White individuals. This indicates a significant disparity.

Violent Victimization Rates (2021)

Source: BJS (NCVS)

Insight: The violent victimization rate for Black individuals was 28.2 per 1,000 persons aged 12 or older, while for White individuals it was 19.7 per 1,000. This shows a higher rate of violent victimization for Black individuals.

Incarceration Rates (2020)

Source: BJS

Insight: In 2020, the incarceration rate for Black residents was 854 per 100,000, compared to 172 per 100,000 for White residents, highlighting a considerable difference.

Poverty Rates (2020)

Source: Census Bureau

Insight: The poverty rate among Black individuals was 22.0% in 2020, while for White individuals it was 8.1%. This indicates a notable disparity in poverty rates.

Confidence in Police (2022)

Source: Pew Research Center

Insight: In 2022, 72% of Black adults reported not having much or no confidence in the police, compared to 37% of White adults, showing a difference in confidence levels.
